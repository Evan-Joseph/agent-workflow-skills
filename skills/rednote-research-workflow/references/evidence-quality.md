# Evidence Quality

Use this reference when RedNote/Xiaohongshu findings may affect a formal decision.

## Evidence tiers

### Strong

- official website
- official rules or handbook
- primary dataset, paper, or source document
- direct platform record from the relevant institution or organizer

Use strong evidence for final claims.

### Medium

- creator with relevant identity and consistent history
- detailed case post with screenshots or documents
- multiple independent posts describing the same pattern
- comments that reveal repeated user questions

Use medium evidence for leads and hypotheses.

### Weak

- unsourced advice
- viral checklists
- copied templates
- "everyone says" posts
- screenshots with unclear origin
- metrics without context

Use weak evidence only as a prompt to verify elsewhere.

## Engagement metrics

Engagement can help prioritize reading, but it does not prove truth.

Interpret with care:

- high saves may mean the content is useful, aspirational, or just broad
- high comments may mean disagreement, confusion, or controversy
- low engagement does not mean low value in a niche topic
- old posts may have outdated rules or platform norms

Record metrics only as context.

## Verification rules

Before using a finding in formal work, ask:

- What exact claim does this support?
- Is the author likely to know?
- Is the note current enough?
- Is there a primary source?
- Could the post be an ad, repost, or personal anecdote?
- Would this claim matter if it were wrong?

If the answer matters, verify against a stronger source.

## Reporting language

Use careful wording:

- "Several posts suggest..."
- "A recurring user concern is..."
- "This is useful as a lead, not proof."
- "The formal requirement still needs official confirmation."

Avoid:

- "RedNote proves..."
- "The official rule is..."
- "Everyone does this..."
- "This is the standard..."

unless a primary source supports the claim.
